7. Research Agent Briefing

This section defines the research queries that parallel agents should execute BEFORE the report is compiled. Each agent investigates one dimension independently, then findings are synthesized into the report above.

Agent A — Market & Industry Research

Goal: Understand the market COMPANY operates in — size, growth, competitors, trends, regulatory environment.

Research COMPANY and its industry for the following: 1. Total addressable market size and growth rate (cite sources). 2. Top 5 direct competitors — what they do, pricing, strengths/weaknesses. 3. Search Reddit, Quora, Twitter/X, Hacker News for community sentiment about this industry — what are people complaining about? What do they want that they can't get? 4. Identify 3-5 macro trends affecting this industry (regulation, tech shifts, consumer behavior). 5. Where does COMPANY sit in the competitive landscape? Premium/budget/niche? Return findings organized with sources for each claim.

Agent B — Workflow & Operations Analysis

Goal: Map COMPANY's end-to-end workflow, identify bottlenecks, and find automation opportunities.

Analyze COMPANY's operational workflow: 1. Map the end-to-end process from customer inquiry to final delivery. List every step, who does it, and which tool(s) they use. 2. Identify the top 5 bottlenecks — where does work pile up? Where are the manual copy-paste handoffs? 3. For each bottleneck, estimate time lost per week and whether it could be automated. 4. List all software tools in use. Which ones integrate? Which don't? Where is data duplicated? 5. What would an ideal automated workflow look like for this business? Return findings with concrete examples and estimated time/cost savings.

Agent C — Technology & Stack Assessment

Goal: Evaluate COMPANY's current technology, find gaps, and propose tooling improvements.

Assess COMPANY's technology stack: 1. Catalog every software tool, platform, and service in use (website, CRM, payments, fulfillment, communication, docs). 2. Evaluate each tool: is it the right fit? Are there better alternatives? What are common complaints about each tool from the community? 3. Identify missing integrations — where should data flow automatically but doesn't? 4. Research community recommendations for this industry's tech stack (Reddit r/smallbusiness, Indie Hackers, industry forums). 5. Propose a target tech stack with specific tools, estimated costs, and migration complexity. Return findings with tool comparisons (pros/cons/costs).

Agent D — Customer Experience & Revenue

Goal: Understand the customer journey, where revenue leaks, and how to improve retention.

Research COMPANY's customer experience and revenue model: 1. Map the customer journey from discovery → purchase → fulfillment → follow-up. What is the experience at each stage? 2. Identify where customers drop off or experience friction. What's the most common complaint? 3. Analyze the revenue model: What are the revenue streams? Pricing? Average order value? Customer lifetime value? 4. Research community discussions about similar businesses — what pricing models work? What do customers expect? 5. Propose 3 ways to increase revenue (upsells, retention, new channels) based on industry best practices. Return findings with customer sentiment data and revenue impact estimates.

Agent E — Risk & Compliance

Goal: Identify business risks, regulatory requirements, and compliance gaps.

Assess COMPANY's risk and compliance posture: 1. What regulations apply to this industry? (data privacy, payment processing, shipping, industry-specific) 2. What are the top business risks? (single points of failure, key-person dependency, supply chain, seasonal volatility) 3. Research common legal/compliance pitfalls for businesses in this space (Reddit, Quora, legal forums). 4. What insurance, contracts, or terms of service are standard in this industry? 5. What happens if the owner/founder is unavailable for 2 weeks? What breaks? Return findings with risk severity ratings and mitigation recommendations.
